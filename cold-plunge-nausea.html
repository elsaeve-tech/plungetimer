Cold Plunge Nausea

Feeling nauseous during or after a cold plunge can happen, especially when your body is adjusting to cold exposure.

Can Cold Plunging Cause Nausea?

Yes, cold plunging can sometimes cause nausea. This is often due to the body's stress response, changes in breathing, and rapid shifts in circulation.

Why It Happens

When you enter cold water, your body activates a strong stress response. Breathing can become rapid, and blood flow shifts toward your core. These changes can make some people feel lightheaded or nauseous.

Common Triggers

  • Entering the water too quickly
  • Cold temperatures that are too intense
  • Holding your breath or breathing irregularly
  • Staying in longer than your body can tolerate

How to Reduce Nausea

  • Focus on slow, controlled breathing
  • Start with shorter durations
  • Use slightly warmer water if needed
  • Enter gradually instead of all at once
