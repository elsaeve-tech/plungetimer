Cold Plunge and Heart Rate

Cold water immersion causes an immediate increase in heart rate due to the cold shock response. Breathing becomes faster, and the cardiovascular system reacts quickly.

What Happens in Cold Water?

When entering cold water, the body activates the sympathetic nervous system. This increases heart rate and blood pressure temporarily.

Does Heart Rate Go Down After?

After the initial response, many people experience a calming effect as the body adapts. This can lead to a slower heart rate and a relaxed state post-plunge.

Safety Considerations

People with cardiovascular conditions should be cautious with cold exposure, especially sudden immersion.
