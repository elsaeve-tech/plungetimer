How Long to Cold Plunge (Simple Guide by Temperature)

In general, colder water calls for shorter sessions, while slightly milder cold allows longer exposure.

For a full breakdown by experience level, see our main guide: How long should a cold plunge be?

Simple Cold Plunge Duration Ranges

  • 35–40°F: about 30–90 seconds
  • 41–45°F: about 45 seconds to 2 minutes
  • 46–50°F: about 1–3 minutes
  • 51–55°F: about 1.5–4 minutes
  • 56–65°F: about 2–5 minutes

What Affects Cold Plunge Time?

Water temperature matters most, but your experience level, comfort, and reason for cold plunging also affect how long you may want to stay in.

Beginners usually do best with shorter, more controlled sessions. It is better to stay conservative and build tolerance gradually over time.

If cold plunging gives you headaches or dizziness, your sessions may be too cold or too long. Read: Why Do I Get a Headache After a Cold Plunge?
