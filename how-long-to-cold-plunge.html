How Long Should You Cold Plunge?

Cold plunge duration depends mainly on water temperature and experience level.

In general, colder water requires shorter exposure times. Beginners typically start with shorter plunges and gradually increase duration as their tolerance improves.

Typical Cold Plunge Duration

  • Very cold water (35–40°F): about 30–90 seconds
  • Cold water (41–45°F): about 45 seconds to 2 minutes
  • Moderate cold (46–50°F): about 1–3 minutes
  • Cool water (51–55°F): about 1.5–4 minutes
  • Milder cold (56–65°F): about 2–5 minutes

Factors That Affect Cold Plunge Time

Your experience level, tolerance, and recovery goals can all influence how long you stay in cold water. Many people also practice breathing techniques to stay calm and steady during a plunge.
